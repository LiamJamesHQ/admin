      Our approach

      Why we give windows rather than fixed dates

      Every installation is different, and that difference is the point. If the project immediately before yours needs additional groundwork or unforeseen site work, our team stays until it is done properly.

      That same protection extends to you. When your build needs an extra day to be right, it gets one.

      A realistic window means the work is finished properly. A fixed date means somebody's build gets rushed, and one day it would be yours.

      Changes to your build

      The point of no return is materials

      Adjustments are straightforward until your materials are commissioned. Once timber is cut to your dimensions and bespoke components are on order, a change means re-ordering rather than re-planning, and additional cost usually follows.

      If you are weighing up a change, tell us now rather than later.

      Common questions

      Answers to what most people ask

      Can I still change my order?

      Adjustments are usually possible before materials are commissioned. Afterwards, changes may not be feasible, and where they are, additional cost typically applies. Ask us as soon as the thought occurs.

      When will I hear from you?

      Around the six week mark, unless something needs your attention sooner. We contact customers at genuine milestones rather than sending routine updates that say nothing new.

      Can I have weekly updates?

      Every project is bespoke, so weekly reporting tends to produce a lot of noise and very little signal. We would rather contact you when something has actually moved. You are welcome to ask at any point.

      Could my installation window shift?

      Occasionally, yes. Weather, a preceding installation running long, or a supplier lead time can all move things. Anything significant, you will hear from us.

      When is the balance due?

      Your £500 deposit secures your place in the order queue. The balance is due before delivery, and we will set out the exact schedule with you well ahead of time.
